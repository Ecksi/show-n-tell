Kung Fu

It means, "supreme skill from hard work." A great poet has reached kung fu. The painter, the calligrapher, they can be said to have kung fu. Even the cook, the one who sweeps steps or a masterful servant can have kung fu. Practice.

Preparation. Endless repetition. Until your mind is weary, and your bones ache. Until you're too tired to sweat, to wasted to breathe. That is the way, the only way one acquires kung fu.

Kung Fu applies directly to programming. To become a supreme programmer, one must put in hard work. Endless repetition, trial and error, and struggle. Hours on the keys, reading docs, making wire frames, looking for semi-colons.

Qi Gong

If Kung Fu is for training the outside of the body, Qi Gong is for training the inside. Qigong involves moving meditation, deep rythmic breathing, and calm meditative state of mind.
